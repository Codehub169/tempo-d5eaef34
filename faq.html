Frequently Asked Questions

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept a wide range of vision and medical insurance plans. Please contact our office directly with your insurance details, and our staff will be happy to verify your coverage and benefits.

How can I prepare for my first eye exam?

To prepare for your exam, please bring your current glasses or contact lenses, a list of any medications you are taking, and your insurance information. If you have a family history of eye conditions, it's helpful to mention that as well.

What is the difference between an Optometrist and an Ophthalmologist?

An Optometrist (OD) provides primary vision care, including eye exams, prescriptions for glasses/contacts, and diagnosis of common eye diseases. An Ophthalmologist (MD) is a medical doctor who specializes in eye and vision care, trained to perform eye surgery, and treat more complex eye conditions.

Do you offer emergency eye care?

Yes, for acute eye injuries, sudden vision changes, or severe pain, please call our office immediately. We prioritize emergency cases to ensure prompt and appropriate care.

Can I fill out patient forms online?

Yes, to save time during your visit, you can download and print our patient registration forms from our website's 'Patient Forms' section (link below) and fill them out prior to your appointment.
